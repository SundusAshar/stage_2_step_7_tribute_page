Abdul Sattar Edhi

Founder of World's largest social welfare organisation, Edhi Foundation

Timeline of Important Events in Edhi's Life!

  • 1928 - Born in Bantwa, India
  • 1939 - Took responsibility of caring his paralysed and mentally ill mother. His personal experience made him think of thousands and millions, suffering like his mother. He had a vision of chains of welfare centres and hospitals that could be opened to alleviate the pain of those suffering from illness and neglect.
  • 1947 - Migrated to Karachi, Pakistan. In order to earn his living, Edhi initially started as a peddler, later became a commission agent selling cloth in the wholesale market in Karachi.
  • 1951 - He began his humaniterian work and decided to establish a free dispensary. He became involved in this charity work. However, soon his personal vision of a growing and developing system of multifarious services made him decide to establish a welfare trust of his own and named it as “Edhi Trust”.
  • 1957 - Edhi's charitable activities expanded greatly when an Asian flu epidemic (originating in China) swept through Pakistan and the rest of the world. Donations allowed him to buy his first ambulance the same year.
  • 1965 - Abdul Sattar Edhi married Bilquis Bano, a nurse who worked at the Edhi dispensary.
  • 1986 - Received Ramon Magsaysay Award for Public Service.
  • 1988 - Received Lenin Peace Prize.
  • 1997 - Pakistan's Edhi Foundation, is listed in the Guinness Book of World Records for running the world's largest ambulance network founded by Abdul Sattar Edhi.
  • 2010 - He was awarded Honorary Doctorate by the University of Bedfordshire.
  • 2011 - He received London Peace Award.
  • 2016 - He died at the age of 88.

Edhi Foundation now runs the world's largest volunteer ambulance service (operating over 1,500 of them) and offers 24-hour emergency services.
